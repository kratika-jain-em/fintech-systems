# PSD2 -> PSD3/PSR - The Engineering Series

> **This is an in-progress series.** 

Articles are being added incrementally. The intended structure and scope are described below, but not every article is published yet.

---

## What this series covers

A structured set of technical articles on EU payment services regulation, written from an engineering perspective. PSD2 remains the current framework. PSD3 and its companion regulation, PSR, reached political agreement in November 2025 and are expected to reshape how payment institutions, e-money institutions, open banking providers, and engineering teams operate across the EU.

This series focuses on what that means in practice:
- system design decisions
- API architecture
- authentication and consent flows
- fraud controls
- operational and compliance-aware engineering obligations

It is not a legal summary, but written for people who need to build, assess, or operate payment systems correctly.

---

## Who this is for

This series is intended for:
- engineers building payment or open banking systems
- engineering managers and architects in regulated fintech
- product, compliance, and operations stakeholders working closely with engineering
- advisors or technical leaders assessing regulatory and engineering readiness

It is especially relevant for teams working on payment initiation, account information services, authentication, consent management, and regulated API infrastructure across the EU and UK.

## Accuracy and Source Standards

Each article in this series aims to:
- distinguish clearly between current law, expected changes, and still-moving proposals
- prioritise primary sources over commentary where possible
- include a **Last updated** date so time-sensitive points can be read in context

> PSD3 and PSR are not yet published in the EU Official Journal. References to their expected provisions, timelines, or implementation implications are based on
> the political agreement of November 2025 and other publicly available legislative material, and may change once the final texts are adopted and published.
